**Executive Summary**
As of April 2, 2026, West Asia remains engulfed in escalating conflict, primarily driven by the US-Israel war against Iran, codenamed Operation Epic Fury, which commenced on February 28, 2026. This has triggered widespread regional instability, including renewed hostilities in Lebanon, fragile ceasefires in Gaza, Houthi involvement from Yemen, and disruptions to global trade via the Strait of Hormuz and Red Sea. Diplomatic efforts, including UN Security Council resolutions and US ceasefire proposals, face significant hurdles amid ongoing military actions. Humanitarian crises persist across Gaza, Lebanon, and Iran, with thousands dead and millions displaced.

**Overview**
The region is witnessing its most severe escalation since the 2023-2025 Israel-Hamas/Hezbollah wars. US and Israeli strikes have targeted Iran's nuclear sites, missile facilities, leadership (including Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei), and infrastructure, prompting Iranian retaliation against US bases in Gulf states, Israel, and oil facilities. President Donald Trump has indicated the US could conclude operations in 2-3 weeks, but no end date is confirmed. Global impacts include halted Middle East flights, shipping reroutes, and surging oil prices. UNSC Resolution 2817 (March 11, 2026) condemned Iran's attacks on Gulf states and demanded compliance with international law.

**Key Conflicts**

1. **US-Israel-Iran War**
   - Initiated February 28 with ~900 strikes killing Khamenei and top officials.
   - Iran responded with missiles/drones on US assets in UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, etc., and Israel.
   - Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, causing fuel shortages in Asia and global oil spikes.
   - Iran appointed Mojtaba Khamenei as new Supreme Leader; IRGC hardliners dominate.
   - Casualties: Thousands in Iran, Lebanon, Israel, Gulf states; civilian sites hit on both sides.

2. **Israel-Hezbollah (Lebanon)**
   - Ceasefire from 2024 broken; Hezbollah launched attacks post-Iran strikes.
   - Israel conducting air strikes up to Beirut, ground invasion of southern Lebanon (March 17 onward), plans to occupy up to Litani River.
   - Over 1 million Lebanese displaced; IDF soldier killed; sectarian tensions rising.

3. **Gaza and Occupied Palestinian Territory**
   - Fragile ceasefire under US 'Comprehensive Plan' (UNSC Res. 2803, Nov 2025): Hostages returned, partial Israeli withdrawal.
   - Board of Peace (BoP, chaired by Trump) overseeing interim technocratic government; $17B pledged for reconstruction (UN estimates $70B).
   - Periodic violence: 689 Palestinians killed since ceasefire. Kerem Shalom only aid crossing open.
   - West Bank: Settler violence displaced 1,697 Palestinians YTD; settlements expanding.

4. **Yemen (Houthis) and Syria**
   - Houthis joined March 28 with missile/drone attacks on Israel; threats to Red Sea shipping.
   - Syria's post-Assad recovery (Dec 2024) at risk from spillover; limited direct involvement reported.

**De-escalation Efforts**
- **UNSC**: Res. 2817 demands Iran halt attacks; Russian ceasefire draft failed. April quarterly debate upcoming (Bahrain presiding).
- **US Proposals**: 15-point ceasefire plan (via Pakistan) offers nuclear curbs, sanctions relief for 1-month pause; rejected by Iran, who submitted counter-proposal.
- **Mediators**: Oman attempted talks pre-war; UK rallying 30+ nations to reopen Hormuz.
- **Gaza-Specific**: BoP/ISF (20K troops pledged) negotiating Hamas decommissioning linked to Israeli withdrawal; Hamas unresponsive.
- International pledges for Gaza stabilization force delayed (e.g., Indonesia on hold).

**Outlook**
Prospects for de-escalation remain dim. US aims for quick resolution but signals possible regime influence in Iran. Iran's horizontal escalation strategy prolongs conflict. Gaza ceasefire vulnerable to spillover; West Bank tensions exacerbate Palestinian fragmentation. Economic pressures (oil, shipping) may force talks, but hardliners dominate. UNSC divisions (China/Russia abstentions) hinder unified action. Monitoring Trump's April addresses and April 2026 UN debates critical.
