## Visa Requirements & Processing Times

### 🇺🇸 United States (Seattle) - CRITICAL

**Requirement:** B1/B2 Tourist Visa (MANDATORY)  
**Fee:** $185 USD (~₹15,500 INR)  
**Processing Time:** **7.5-13 MONTHS** (varies by city)

**Current Wait Times by City (as of September 2025):**
- Chennai: ~9-14 months
- Delhi: Up to 12-13 months
- Kolkata: ~5-6 months
- Mumbai: ~7-9 months

**Important Notes:**
- ⚠️ **Apply IMMEDIATELY** - New rules effective September 6, 2025 require applying in country of citizenship
- ⚠️ Third-country applications no longer allowed
- ⚠️ Plan for potential 221(g) administrative processing delays (additional weeks/months)
- ⚠️ Interview appointment availability varies weekly

**Required Documents:**
- Valid passport (6+ months beyond intended stay)
- DS-160 confirmation page
- Appointment confirmation
- Photo meeting specifications
- Financial documents (bank statements, income proof)
- Travel itinerary
- Employment/study verification letters

**Recommendation:** If you don't have a valid US visa yet, apply TODAY. Your travel window (June 2026) may be tight depending on current wait times.

### 🇭🇰 Hong Kong (For Stopover)

**Requirement:** Pre-arrival Registration (PAR) - FREE  
**Validity:** Up to 14 days visa-free stay after PAR approval  
**Processing Time:** **Instant to 24 hours** (automatic online system)

**Application Process:**
1. Visit official Immigration Department website
2. Complete online PAR form
3. Upload passport scan
4. Provide onward travel details (Seattle flight confirmation)
5. Receive instant approval notification

**Documents Needed:**
- Valid Indian passport (6+ months validity)
- Proof of onward travel to Seattle (confirmed flight ticket)
- Email address for notification
- Credit/debit card (for any future fees, though PAR itself is free)

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Completely free
- ✅ Instant approval
- ✅ Simple online process
- ✅ Allows full 14-day stay (more than enough for 2-3 day stopover)

**Official Website:** https://www.immd.gov.hk/eng/services/visas/pre-arrival_registration_for_indian_nationals.html

**Requirement:** Schengen Visa (Type C) if leaving airport during layover  
**Fee:** €80 (~₹7,200)  
**Validity:** Up to 90 days in 180-day period  
**Processing Time:** 15 calendar days minimum (can extend to 45 days)

- Required even if NOT leaving airport (for Indian citizens)
- Fee: €80
- Allows only transit through international zone
- Cannot leave airport premises

**Important:** If you want to explore Frankfurt, London, Paris, etc. during your stopover, you'll need a full Schengen tourist visa, which requires:
- Hotel bookings
- Travel insurance (€30,000 coverage minimum)
- Flight itineraries
- Financial proof
- Cover letter explaining purpose

### Booking Strategy

**Option A: Single Ticket (Convenience)**
- Book entire journey (BLR-HKG-SEA) on one ticket
- Pros: Baggage checked through, rebooking protection if delays occur
- Cons: Less flexibility, potentially higher cost
- Best for: First-time travelers, those wanting simplicity

**Option B: Separate Tickets (Flexibility)**
- Book BLR-HKG separately, then HKG-SEA separately
- Pros: Maximum flexibility, can adjust stopover duration, potentially lower cost
- Cons: Need to collect baggage in HKG, no protection if first flight delayed
- Best for: Experienced travelers, those wanting budget control

**Recommendation:** For your first trip, consider Option A for peace of mind. If budget is critical, Option B works well with Cathay Pacific's reliable schedule.

### Fare Classes to Consider

Given geopolitical uncertainty, consider:
1. **Flexible/Flex fares** - Allow changes/cancellations with minimal fees
2. **Refundable tickets** - Peace of mind for unexpected situations
3. **Travel insurance** covering trip cancellation due to geopolitical events

### Price Monitoring Tips

1. Set up Google Flights price alerts for BLR-SEA routes
2. Check prices weekly for 2-3 months before booking
3. Book when you see a good price (don't wait for "perfect" price)
4. Consider booking return flight separately if outbound price is right but return is uncertain
